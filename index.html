Ohm's law and Watt's law
V = volts, I = current (amps), R = resistance (ohms), P = power (watts). Every quantity can be found from any two of the others.
To findFormulas
VI × RP / I√(P × R)
IV / RP / V√(P / R)
RV / IV² / PP / I²
PV × II² × RV² / R
Resistance
SeriesR_total = R1 + R2 + ... + Rn
Parallel1/R_total = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + ... + 1/Rn
(two resistors: R_total = (R1 × R2) / (R1 + R2))
Capacitance
Series1/C_total = 1/C1 + 1/C2 + ... + 1/Cn
ParallelC_total = C1 + C2 + ... + Cn
Charge storedQ = C × V
Energy storedE = ½ × C × V²
Capacitive reactanceXc = 1 / (2 × π × f × C)
Inductance
SeriesL_total = L1 + L2 + ... + Ln
Parallel1/L_total = 1/L1 + 1/L2 + ... + 1/Ln
Energy storedE = ½ × L × I²
Inductive reactanceXL = 2 × π × f × L
Time constants
RC circuitτ = R × C
RL circuitτ = L / R
f = frequency (Hz), τ (tau) = time constant in seconds.
